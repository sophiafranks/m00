Intro to Web Development - Current

PROG109 - Adam Brehm
In this course, I have learned how to code in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to develop my own website. There have been a lot of ups and downs during this course. The most notable one was when I got sick for a solid 3 weeks. Keeping up with some of the material was hard, but finally solving some of the issues I came across felt incredibly rewarding. I think the largest takeaway from this course will be that I now feel like I have a solid foundation of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript skills.

Intro to Hand Power Tools - Spring Quarter

ART108 - Tahle Patton
This was a half-quarter class where I learned how to use various power tools to create a bench of my own design. Unfortunately, I was not able to attend the final class because that was when I fell terribly ill. I am sad that I missed out on that.

American Government - Winter Quarter

POLS202 - Tim Jones
In this course, I learned about our own system of government, its history, intricacies, and many different political ideologies. I also kept up with the current news. This class was also a challenge for me due to the sheer amount of work required every week. I remember that the midterm overlapped with my visit to San Jose to visit my sister. I immediately caught Covid. I was completely miserable; studying for an already daunting midterm while in an unfamiliar space while I was sick out of my mind was not fun. Luckily, I ended up getting the highest score on that midterm out of the entire class (95/100). After that, I felt like I could conquer the entire world! I think this class was the right kind of hard. I was forced to learn the material, and I feel like I gained a lot from that. I still find myself using knowledge I gained from this class months later.

US History III - Winter Quarter

HIST148 - Polly Good
In this course, I learned more about US History. I cannot remember much from this course, except that I enjoyed a lot of the documentaries we were assigned to watch. There was one documentary on YouTube that covered the Panama Canal. I vividly remember being shocked at how one man, William Gorgas, decided to get rid of Yellow Fever. He ended up waging war on the mosquito population. He would have stagnant water covered in oil so they couldn't reproduce. Anyone who had stagnant water and therefore mosquito larvae would be fined 5$, and he would hunt down individual mosquitos. This worked very well as the number of people who came down with Yellow Fever drastically decreased.

Modern Architecture and Design - Summer Quarter

ART101 - Deborah Leblang
In this course, I learned about Modern Architecture and Design. I remember for one of the major assignments, I had to research a famous architect of my choosing. I ended up stumbling into a biography by a friend of one of the architects I chose, Bertram Grosvenor Goodhue. One of the quotes read as follows:

"I remember him best in two aspects: sitting hunched up over a drawing-board, his lips writing nervously around innumerable dropping cigarettes, shifting his pen from one hand to another (he was ambidextrous) as he wrought out some inimitable study in dazzling black and white ... He was Bertram Grosvenor Goodhue, and in saying that you have said all."

I remember late at night, crying over this quote. It, for some reason, struck a nerve in me. That is why I wanted to share it here.

Survey of Human Disease - Spring Quarter

BIOL125 - Jean Lawler
In this course, I learned a lot about both the normal functions of the body, as well as the different ways that diseases manifest and disrupt those normal functions. This class was fast paced, with only written lectures being available. It involved a lot of memorization, which I did not entirely enjoy. There were some assignments where we would draw and explain diagrams or research some basic information, but they were not the focus. I learned a lot through this class, and I am glad I took it.

Contemporary Moral Issues - Spring Quarter

PHIL102 - Greg A. Damico
In this course, I learned a lot about different Philosophers, contemporary moral issues, and the logic surrounding the different approaches and solutions to those issues. I really liked this class. I remember when I took my final, I had the same song on repeat for the entire 3 hours. Now, whenever I listen to that song, I suddenly feel like I am taking the exam again. This class certainly challenged me, but I certainly enjoyed it.

US History I - Winter Quarter

I really connected with the primary and secondary sources that were presented in the class, and it led me to do a lot of reflection on the current state of the US.
